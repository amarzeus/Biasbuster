Essential Media Literacy Resources

Evaluating Sources

Learn how to critically evaluate news sources and information for credibility, bias, and reliability using the ESCAPE method.

  • Evidence - Is it based on facts?
  • Source - Who created this information?
  • Context - What's the bigger picture?
  • Audience - Who is this meant for?
  • Purpose - Why was this created?
  • Execution - How is it presented?

Develop skills to identify false or misleading information online, including deepfakes, manipulated media, and AI-generated content.

  • Spotting clickbait headlines
  • Recognizing emotional manipulation
  • Identifying false context

  • Cross-checking information

Deepfake Detection Challenge

Learn to identify potentially manipulated media by recognizing common signs of deepfakes and other forms of synthetic media.

How to Spot Potential Deepfakes:
  • Check for unnatural facial movements, especially around the eyes and mouth
  • Look for inconsistent lighting, shadows, or skin tone
  • Pay attention to strange blending lines where the face meets the neck or hair
  • Notice audio that doesn't perfectly sync with lip movements
  • Use reverse image search to find the original source of the media
